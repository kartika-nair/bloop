# Bloop!

Bloop is an application that helps you go offline when you've been doomscrolling for too long (even though you said it would only be a couple of minutes). Because we believe that touching grass shouldn't feel like a chore, and should instead feel like levelling up :)

Users begin by signing up and granting permissions for camera access and screen-time monitoring.
They are then introduced to Bloop, a cute mushroom companion who starts with a full health bar. As the user spends more time on their phone, Bloop’s health gradually decreases — visually reflecting their screen usage.
When screen time approaches a user-defined limit, the app sends a warning notification 10 seconds before the limit is reached. If usage continues beyond the threshold, the app overlays the current screen: it slowly fades to black, and the user is prompted to “Redeem Yourself.”
Tapping “Redeem Yourself” takes the user to Bloop, who is now visibly low on health. To regain control of their phone and restore Bloop’s wellbeing, the user must complete a real-world quest designed to encourage offline activity.
Quests include activities such as:
- Saying hello to a stranger
- Taking a walk in the park
- Taking a photo of the sky
Other small, meaningful offline interactions

To verify completion, users can upload a photo as proof, which can be analyzed using on-device machine learning. If the quest involves another Bloop user, a unique QR code can be generated and scanned for mutual verification.
Once the quest is approved, Bloop’s health is restored, allowing it to thrive — and giving the user full access to their phone again.
